LEAVE AND ATTENDANCE POLICY

1. Objective

This policy establishes the guidelines for all types of leave, attendance expectations, and procedures for handling absences—including AWOL (Absent Without Leave)—while ensuring full compliance with the Philippine Labor Code and related laws.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees of Digital Minds BPO, regardless of status (probationary, regular, or contractual).

3. Definitions
  • Leave: Authorized time away from work.
  • Leave Credits: Paid leave days granted or accrued by an employee.
  • SIL (Service Incentive Leave): Statutory five (5) days paid leave after one year of service.
  • LWOP: Leave Without Pay due to insufficient paid credits.
  • AWOL: Unauthorized absence for two (2) consecutive workdays without notice or approval.
  • Emergency Leave: Unplanned leave due to unforeseen circumstances requiring immediate attention.
4. Eligibility

This policy covers both probationary and regular employees. Statutory leave benefits apply only to those who meet the minimum legal requirements.

5. Salient Features
  • Compliance with all applicable labor laws (Labor Code, RA 11210, Solo Parent Act, Magna Carta for Women, etc.).
  • Two (2) paid company leaves per month for regular employees.
  • Strict documentation requirements for health- or emergency-related absences.
  • Clear AWOL definition and process aligned with due process.
  • Leave filing deadlines to ensure operational continuity.
6. Leave Benefits and Entitlements

6.1 Statutory Leaves

  • Service Incentive Leave: 5 paid days annually after 1 year of service.
  • Maternity Leave: 105 days paid (additional 15 for solo parents) and 60 days for miscarriage.
  • Paternity Leave: 7 days paid for the first 4 deliveries of legitimate spouse.
  • Solo Parent Leave: 7 days per year with Solo Parent ID.
  • Special Gynecological Leave: Up to 2 months paid for covered medical procedures.
  • VAWC Leave: Up to 10 days paid for victims of violence.

6.2 Company-Provided Leaves

  • Paid Leave: Maximum of 2 paid leaves per month for regular employees.
  • Unpaid Leave: Available to all employees subject to approval.
  • Emergency Leave: Must be filed at least 2 hours before the shift.

6.3 Probationary Employees

Not entitled to SIL yet (per law) but may request unpaid leave subject to supervisor approval.

7. Filing Requirements and Timelines
  • Paid Leave (Regular Employees): File at least 5 days before intended date.
  • Unpaid Leave: File at least 3 days before intended date.
  • Emergency Leave: Notify at least 2 hours before shift.
  • Documentation: Medical certificates or formal explanation letters may be required.
  • Approval: Based on operational needs and staffing levels.
8. Leave Application Process
  1. Employee accomplishes the official leave form.
  2. Supervisor validates operational coverage and approves/disapproves.
  3. HR verifies leave credits and documentation.
  4. Leave is recorded in the HRIS and payroll system.
9. Leave Encashment
  • Only SIL is encashable at year-end or upon separation.
  • Company-provided paid leaves are not automatically encashable.
  • Statutory leaves (maternity, paternity, etc.) are non-convertible.

10. AWOL (Absent Without Leave) Policy

An employee is considered AWOL when they are absent for two (2) consecutive workdays without notice, approval, or valid justification.

10.1 Notification Requirements

Employees must notify their Supervisor or HR as soon as possible through:

  • Email
  • Phone or SMS
  • Noysi or official communication platform

10.2 Procedure for Handling AWOL

  1. Initial Contact: Company attempts to reach employee within 24 hours.
  2. Investigation: HR verifies circumstances and documents findings.
  3. Written Notice: Employee receives a formal notification of possible sanctions.

10.3 Consequences

  • First Instance: Written warning.
  • Repeated Instance: Final warning and possible suspension.
  • Two+ Days AWOL: May be subject to termination for job abandonment following due process.

10.4 Appeals

Employees may appeal AWOL decisions by submitting valid justification and supporting documents.

11. Attendance Considerations
  • Attendance directly impacts incentives (e.g., Perfect Attendance, KPI bonuses).
  • Perfect Attendance incentives are prorated based on paid leaves taken.
  • KPI incentives are prorated based on attendance performance.
12. Approving Authority
  • Supervisors/Team Leads: First-level approval.
  • HR Department: Compliance verification.
  • Department Manager: Required for extended or exceptional leave requests.
  • HR Manager: Final authority for statutory or sensitive leave matters.
13. Interpretation

Any ambiguity in this policy shall be interpreted in favor of compliance with the Philippine Labor Code and all applicable laws.
