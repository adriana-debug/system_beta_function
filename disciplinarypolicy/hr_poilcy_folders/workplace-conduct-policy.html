WORKPLACE CONDUCT POLICY

1. Purpose

This policy establishes the standards of professional behavior expected of all individuals within Digital Minds BPO. Its goal is to maintain a respectful, productive, and safe work environment conducive to collaboration, operational excellence, and compliance with legal and ethical requirements.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees—regardless of rank or employment status—as well as contractors, trainees, interns, vendors, service providers, and visitors conducting business within company premises or representing the company in any capacity.

3. Policy Guidelines

3.1 Professionalism

  • Employees must conduct themselves professionally in all interactions with colleagues, clients, customers, and visitors.
  • Professional behavior includes courtesy, respect, fairness, and observing proper communication etiquette.
  • Disruptive, disrespectful, or unprofessional conduct may be subject to disciplinary action under the Company’s Disciplinary Policy.

3.2 Workplace Safety

  • Employees must strictly adhere to all safety rules, emergency procedures, and hazard-prevention protocols.
  • Any unsafe condition, equipment malfunction, hazard, or accident must be promptly reported to HR, the Safety Officer, or direct management.
  • Employees must not engage in actions that endanger themselves or others.

3.3 Attendance and Punctuality

  • Employees are expected to arrive on time and follow their assigned work schedules.
  • Absences, tardiness, undertime, and schedule deviations must be reported in accordance with the Company’s Attendance Policy.
  • Chronic attendance issues may lead to disciplinary measures.

3.4 Confidentiality and Data Security

  • Employees must safeguard confidential and proprietary information, including client data, internal company records, and trade secrets.
  • Unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, or misuse of confidential information is strictly prohibited.
  • Violations may result in disciplinary action, termination, and/or legal liability.

3.5 Workplace Harassment and Discrimination

Digital Minds BPO maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy against harassment, discrimination, bullying, or any inappropriate conduct based on:

  • Race or ethnicity
  • Sex or gender identity
  • Sexual orientation
  • Religion or faith
  • Disability or medical condition
  • Age, marital status, or any other protected characteristic

Employees are encouraged to report any incident to HR or management. Reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly.

3.6 Conflict Resolution

  • Employees are encouraged to resolve disputes amicably, respectfully, and constructively.
  • Conflicts that cannot be resolved informally should be escalated to HR or management for mediation.
  • Hostile, aggressive, or disruptive behavior is strictly prohibited.

3.7 Use of Company Resources

  • Company resources such as computers, internet access, tools, facilities, and supplies must be used responsibly and primarily for business purposes.
  • Unauthorized personal use, abuse, or misuse of resources may result in sanctions.
  • Employees must observe IT security guidelines and refrain from accessing restricted systems or inappropriate content.

3.8 Compliance with Policies and Laws

  • Employees are expected to familiarize themselves with all company policies and comply with them at all times.
  • Violation of any policy—including this Workplace Conduct Policy—may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

4. Reporting and Investigation Procedures
  • Employees may report violations anonymously or directly to HR, their supervisor, or the designated Compliance Officer.
  • All reports will be evaluated promptly and professionally.
  • Investigations will be conducted impartially and confidentially.
  • Retaliation against individuals who report violations in good faith is strictly prohibited.
5. Disciplinary Actions

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action in accordance with the Company’s Disciplinary Policy. Penalties may include:

  • Coaching or counseling
  • Verbal or written warnings
  • Suspension
  • Final warning
  • Termination of employment
6. Exceptions and Interpretation

Any exception to this policy must be approved in writing by HR Management. In cases of ambiguity, the Company reserves the right to interpret and apply the policy in alignment with organizational needs and applicable laws.

7. Effective Date

This Workplace Conduct Policy becomes effective on [Date of Implementation] and supersedes any previous conduct-related guidelines.
