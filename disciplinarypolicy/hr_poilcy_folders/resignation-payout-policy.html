Resignation Process and Final Payout Policy

1. Resignation Notice Period

Employees must submit written notice at least 30 working days before intended last day. Compliance ensures proper transition and handover.

2. Early Release
  • Filled Vacated Slot: Early release possible if replacement is available; requires supervisor and HR approval.
  • Health Reasons: Early release requires medical documentation and HR review.
3. Deduction for Unfulfilled Service Days

Failure to render required service days results in deduction from final pay. Calculated as: Basic rate × number of days not served.

4. Withheld Payouts
  • Reasons: Missing documentation, resignation, or termination.
  • Payout is temporarily withheld for clearance for resigned/terminated employees.
5. Payout Examples
Last Working Day (LWD) Payout Status
August 16 Aug 1–15: Released Aug 20; Aug 16: Hold for clearance
August 21 Aug 16–21: Hold for clearance
August 20 Aug 1–15: Released Aug 20; Aug 16–20: Hold for clearance
August 31 Aug 16–31: Hold for clearance
6. Philippine Labor Law Compliance
  • All deductions and withholding comply with the Labor Code of the Philippines.
  • Employees are entitled to all final pay, less lawful deductions, upon completion of clearance.
