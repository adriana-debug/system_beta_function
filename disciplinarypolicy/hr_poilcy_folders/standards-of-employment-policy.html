STANDARDS OF EMPLOYMENT POLICY

1. Purpose

This policy sets forth the minimum standards of employment expected from employees of Digital Minds BPO Services Inc. These standards ensure a professional, ethical, and efficient workplace aligned with operational goals and compliant with the Labor Code of the Philippines.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals engaged in work for the company, including:

  • Regular Employees
  • Probationary Employees
  • Trainees
  • Contractors and Project-based Personnel
  • Temporary or Agency Staff

This policy covers all on-site, remote, off-site, and client-related work activities.

3. Employer’s Standards of Employment

3.1 External Appearance

Employees must maintain a neat, clean, and professional appearance at all times while representing the company. Compliance with the company's Dress Code Policy is mandatory.

3.2 Attendance & Punctuality

Employees must honor assigned schedules, including shift times, breaks, training sessions, and departmental meetings. Frequent tardiness, undertime, or absences without valid reason or approval may constitute Habitual Neglect of Duty (Labor Code Art. 297) and may lead to disciplinary action.

3.3 Job Competence

Employees are expected to demonstrate role proficiency and continuously develop skills necessary to perform their duties. During probation, job competence is a key criterion for regularization in compliance with Labor Code Art. 296.

3.4 Attitude

Employees must demonstrate professionalism, initiative, and willingness to learn. A cooperative and respectful attitude is required in all interactions with supervisors, peers, and clients.

3.5 Discipline

Employees must follow company rules, internal policies, and lawful orders of superiors. Violations are subject to corrective or disciplinary action following the Twin Notice Rule of due process.

3.6 Interpersonal Relations

Employees are expected to maintain courteous, respectful, and collaborative relationships at work. Bullying, harassment, or discrimination is prohibited under company policy and applicable laws (e.g. Safe Spaces Act, Anti-Sexual Harassment Act).

3.7 Background Checks

Employment is contingent upon passing all security, identity, employment history, and background checks. Providing false or misleading information may be grounds for termination (Labor Code Art. 297 - Fraud).

3.8 Work Quality

Employees must meet or exceed the company’s performance benchmarks, quality standards, and productivity expectations. Consistent poor performance may trigger performance improvement procedures.

3.9 Planning and Organizing

Employees should demonstrate the ability to prioritize tasks, manage workloads, and organize resources effectively to meet deadlines and operational requirements.

3.10 Results Orientation

Employees are expected to deliver results and complete tasks efficiently despite workload challenges. Emphasis is placed on accountability, productivity, and ownership of work outputs.

4. Compliance

All employees are responsible for understanding and adhering to the standards outlined in this policy. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment, following the due process requirements of the Labor Code.

5. Effectivity

This policy is effective immediately upon release and shall remain in force unless amended or superseded by management.
