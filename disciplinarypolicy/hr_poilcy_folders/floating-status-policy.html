FLOATING STATUS POLICY

1. Purpose

This policy provides clear guidelines regarding the placement, treatment, compensation, and reassignment of employees under Floating Status within Digital Minds BPO. It ensures that employees’ rights are protected while operational requirements are managed responsibly and legally.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees who may be temporarily without a campaign assignment due to client changes, account transitions, project ramp-downs, or other operational factors.

3. Definition of Floating Status

Floating Status refers to a temporary employment condition where an employee remains employed by the Company but currently has no assigned campaign or project. The employee is not terminated and remains part of the workforce while awaiting reassignment.

During this period, employees are considered active and may be reassigned once a suitable position becomes available that matches their skills and qualifications.

4. Compensation
  • Employees under Floating Status shall be compensated for the days they physically reported for work or rendered billable hours.
  • Floating Status does not entitle the employee to continuous daily compensation unless work was actually rendered.
  • Payouts will follow the standard company payroll schedule.

Example: If an employee worked from the 1st to the 4th day of the month and entered Floating Status on the 5th, the compensation for the 1st to 4th will be processed and released on the regular payout date (e.g., the 20th).

5. Reassignment to a New Campaign
  1. Skill Assessment: HR and Operations will review the skills, experience, and qualification profiles of employees under Floating Status.
  2. Matching with Available Campaigns: Employees will be prioritized for reassignment based on the alignment of their skills with available opportunities.
  3. Employee Initiative: Employees are encouraged to update their skill sets through training or certification programs to increase their reassignment prospects.
  4. Priority System: Those with the closest match to campaign requirements will be prioritized to ensure operational readiness and campaign success.
6. Communication Requirements
  • Employees in Floating Status must maintain timely communication with their HR representative or assigned supervisor.
  • HR will provide updates regarding available campaigns, reassignment opportunities, or changes in status.
  • Failure to maintain communication may delay reassignment or lead to potential misunderstandings in employment status.

7. Exit Procedures
  1. If an employee under Floating Status voluntarily opts to leave the company, they must follow all standard exit procedures.
  2. Employees must:
    • Submit their formal resignation with proper notice,
    • Return company assets and property,
    • Complete the exit interview process.
  3. Exit interviews help the Company improve the employee experience, including for those exiting from Floating Status.
8. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

Employees under Floating Status remain bound by all existing confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations. These responsibilities continue uninterrupted even during periods without campaign assignment.

Employees must protect company and client data at all times. Unauthorized disclosure may result in disciplinary or legal action.

9. Employee Responsibilities While on Floating Status
  • Remain reachable through updated contact information.
  • Report for training, evaluation, or meetings when instructed by HR or Operations.
  • Actively participate in upskilling opportunities offered by the Company.
10. Company Rights and Limitations
  • The Company reserves the right to determine the duration of Floating Status in compliance with Philippine labor regulations.
  • The Company may reassign employees to suitable roles as soon as operationally feasible.
  • This policy shall be interpreted in accordance with the Labor Code and DOLE guidelines related to temporary layoffs, lack of assignment, and floating arrangements.
11. Effective Date

This policy takes effect on [Date of Implementation] and supersedes any prior guidelines on Floating Status or No-Assignment Procedures.
