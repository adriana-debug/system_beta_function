CRITICAL WORK DAY POLICY

1. Definition

Critical Work Days are company-specified days in the calendar year that are essential for the company to maintain operations. Employees are expected to be present during these days to ensure optimum support for clients and maintain service level standards. Complete staffing ensures smooth operations and reduces the risk of business losses.

2. Rationale

Maintaining work schedules on critical days ensures seamless coverage and efficient delivery of work. Deviations from assigned schedules can result in gaps in coverage, increasing the workload of compliant employees and potentially affecting client service and business operations.

3. Coverage

The following days are considered Critical Work Days:

  • The day before, during, and after local holidays
  • The day during and after the payday
  • The day before and after a requested holiday/vacation leave
  • The day on a previously denied leave
  • The day after corrective suspension

The following positions are covered under this policy:

  • All rank and file employees of the operations department
  • All supervisors and managers under the operations department
  • All key support departments related to business operations (e.g., IT, Maintenance)
4. Ground Rules

Any employee who fails to report on a Critical Work Day (regardless of duration) must submit a medical certificate or other legal documentation to validate the absence. Failure to provide such documentation will result in the absence being classified as UNAUTHORIZED, and the employee may face disciplinary action up to termination.

First Offense Second Offense
Final Written Warning Dismissal

Leave applications for Critical Work Days will be automatically denied, and previously approved leaves are recalled and cancelled. Any unavoidable leave must be requested at least one (1) week prior to the Critical Work Day.

5. Implementation

The Human Resource Department shall announce upcoming Critical Work Days at least two (2) weeks in advance. Immediate supervisors are responsible for forwarding any submitted medical certificates or supporting documents to HR for validation.

6. Effectivity

This policy is effective immediately upon release.
