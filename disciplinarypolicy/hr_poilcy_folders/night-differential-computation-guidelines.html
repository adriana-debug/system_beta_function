GUIDELINES FOR COMPUTATION OF NIGHT DIFFERENTIAL PAY

1. Purpose

This guideline outlines the correct computation of Night Differential (ND) pay for employees working between 10:00 PM and 6:00 AM, in accordance with Article 86 of the Labor Code of the Philippines, which mandates that employees are entitled to at least 10% additional compensation for work performed during night shift hours.

2. Definition

Night Differential Pay is the additional compensation received by employees who render work during officially designated night hours (10 PM to 6 AM). This applies to:

  • Ordinary working days
  • Special Non-Working Holidays
  • Regular Holidays

Computations may vary based on holiday multipliers as mandated by the Labor Code.

3. Computations

3.1 Ordinary Day Night Shift

Conditions: Work rendered between 10 PM and 6 AM on an ordinary day.

Formula:

Ordinary Day Night Differential = (Hourly Rate × 10% × 8 hours)

Example:

Hourly Rate = Php 62.50

Computation:

Php 62.50 × 0.10 × 8 = Php 50.00

3.2 Special Holiday Night Shift

Conditions: Work rendered between 10 PM and 6 AM on a Special Non-Working Holiday.

Formula:

Special Holiday Night Differential = (Hourly Rate × 130% × 10% × 8 hours)

Example:

Hourly Rate = Php 62.50

Computation:

Php 62.50 × 1.3 × 0.10 × 8 = Php 65.00

3.3 Regular Holiday Night Shift

Conditions: Work rendered between 10 PM and 6 AM on a Regular Holiday.

Formula:

Regular Holiday Night Differential = (Hourly Rate × 200% × 10% × 8 hours)

Example:

Hourly Rate = Php 62.50

Computation:

Php 62.50 × 2 × 0.10 × 8 = Php 100.00
4. Legal Basis

This guideline is issued pursuant to Article 86 of the Labor Code of the Philippines, which mandates night shift differential pay, as well as DOLE Wage Orders regarding computation of holiday pay and premium rates.

5. Compliance

All computations are to be followed by Payroll, HR, Operations, and all employees. Any deviation from the prescribed guidelines requires written approval from HR and the Finance Department.

6. Effectivity

This guideline takes effect immediately upon release and remains valid until amended or superseded.
