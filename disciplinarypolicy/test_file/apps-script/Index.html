EMPLOYEE DISCIPLINARY POLICY AND CODE OF CONDUCT

1. Objective

This policy establishes a fair, consistent, and clear framework for handling employee misconduct and promoting an orderly work environment.

2. Rationale

The policy ensures disciplinary actions are imposed in a manner compliant with each employee's right to security of tenure and the due process requirements mandated by the Philippine Labor Code (as renumbered) and DOLE rules. It also promotes transparency, predictability, and proportionality in sanctions to uphold trust across Digital Minds BPO.

3. Effective Date

This policy is effective beginning [Date of Implementation, e.g., January 1, 2026], and supersedes all previous disciplinary codes, rules, and procedures.

4. Salient Features
  • Management Prerogative: The Company reserves the right to prescribe reasonable rules and regulations necessary for the conduct of its business and to impose disciplinary measures.
  • Principle of Proportionality: The penalty imposed shall be commensurate to the gravity of the offense committed, giving due consideration to mitigating, aggravating, or analogous circumstances.
  • Due Process: No employee shall be subjected to disciplinary action without first being accorded the right to substantive (just/authorized cause) and procedural due process (Twin Notice Rule and Ample Opportunity to be Heard).
5. Eligibility

This policy applies to all employees of Digital Minds BPO, regardless of their employment status (e.g., probationary, regular, contractual, or managerial).

6. Procedure for Imposing Disciplinary Action (Just Causes)

Disciplinary action for offenses constituting a Just Cause under the Labor Code shall follow the Twin Notice Rule:

  1. First Written Notice (Notice to Explain/Charge Sheet): The Company shall issue a written notice to the employee, detailing the specific charges, acts or omissions constituting the offense, and the company rule or Labor Code provision violated. The employee shall be given at least five (5) calendar days from receipt to submit a written explanation, and to request a formal hearing/conference, if desired.
  2. Ample Opportunity to be Heard: After receipt of the explanation, or lapse of the period to explain, the Company shall conduct an investigation, and a hearing/conference if requested or deemed necessary to clarify factual issues.
  3. Second Written Notice (Notice of Decision/Termination): After due consideration of all evidence, including the employee's explanation, the Company shall issue a second written notice informing the employee of the final decision (e.g., dismissal, suspension, or exoneration). This notice must be served upon the employee.
7. Preventive Suspension (Non-Punitive)

An employee may be placed under Preventive Suspension (PS) if their continued presence poses a serious and imminent threat to the life or property of the employer or co-employees, or if it may interfere with the investigation. It is not a penalty.

  • Duration: The maximum period for PS is thirty (30) calendar days.
  • Extension: If the investigation is not concluded within 30 days, the employee must be reinstated or the PS may be extended, provided that the extension is with pay, to avoid claims of constructive dismissal.
8. Case Matrix and Sanctions

Sanctions are generally progressive, but the Company retains the discretion to impose a heavier penalty, including immediate dismissal, for a first offense if warranted by the gravity of the violation, damage caused, or other aggravating circumstances (e.g., position of trust, prior offenses).

Group Category of Offense Range of Sanction (Company Discretion)
Group I Minor Infractions (Relatively harmless, first offense rarely causes substantial damage). 1st Offense: Verbal Warning (documented)
2nd Offense: Written Reprimand
3rd Offense: 1 to 3 days Suspension
4th Offense: 4 to 7 days Suspension
5th Offense: Termination (for habitual neglect)
Group II Major Infractions (Affects efficiency, productivity, and internal order but does not automatically warrant dismissal on first offense). 1st Offense: Written Reprimand to 7 days Suspension
2nd Offense: 8 to 30 days Suspension
3rd Offense: Termination
Group III Serious/Gross Offenses (Acts constituting a Just Cause for termination under the Labor Code, or those posing a severe threat to the company). 1st Offense: Immediate Termination (Penalty must be commensurate to the offense).

9. Attendance Related Violations (Examples)
Violation Definition Classification and Note
Tardiness Reporting for work after the officially scheduled start time. Group I (Habitual or excessive tardiness may escalate to Gross and Habitual Neglect of Duty - Group III.)
Undertime Leaving work before the officially scheduled end time without proper authorization. Group I (Habitual or excessive undertime may escalate to Gross and Habitual Neglect of Duty - Group III.)
Unplanned Leave/Absence Absence from work without prior approval but with timely notification to the superior. Group II (Repeated instances without valid justification may be classified as Gross and Habitual Neglect of Duty - Group III.)
Prolonged Unplanned Leave/Absence Absence from work for three (3) or more consecutive working days without prior approval, even with notification. Group II or III, depending on justification. May constitute Abandonment of Work if coupled with a clear intention to sever employment.
No Call, No Show (NCNS) Absence from work without any notification to the Company. Group II (One instance may merit severe suspension. Repeated instances are equivalent to Gross and Habitual Neglect of Duty - Group III.)
Absconding Absence from work for a prolonged period (e.g., more than 5 working days) without leave, coupled with a clear intent to terminate the employment relationship (e.g., failure to report after a warning). Group III (Treated as an analogous cause to Abandonment of Work, a Just Cause for Termination.)
10. Prescriptive Period of Offenses

Disciplinary action must be initiated by the Company within a reasonable time from the discovery of the offense to avoid the legal presumption of condonation.

However, for guidance, the following periods shall be observed:

  • 60 Days: Minor Infractions (Group I).
  • 90 Days: Major Infractions (Group II).
  • 120 Days: Serious/Gross Offenses (Group III) not involving moral turpitude or crime.
  • 365 Days: Offenses resulting in financial damage or those subject to civil/criminal liability.
  • Imprescriptible Offenses (Group III): Offenses that constitute a criminal act against the employer, its family, or representatives (Commission of a Crime), or those involving Fraud or Willful Breach of Trust (e.g., theft, falsification) where the underlying criminal/civil action has a longer prescriptive period. The Company shall be guided by the applicable laws in these cases.

The period shall start from the date the offense was committed or the date the Company or its authorized representative was officially informed/discovered the offense.

11. Procedure for Termination (Just Cause Exception)

The termination process, as detailed in Section 6 (Procedure for Imposing Disciplinary Action), is the standard procedure for all Just Causes under the Labor Code (Art. 297, as renumbered). No employee can be dismissed without the Twin Notice Rule being strictly observed.

Exception to Progressive Sanction: For Group III offenses such as Serious Misconduct, Fraud, Willful Breach of Trust, Gross and Habitual Neglect of Duties, or Commission of a Crime, the Company may impose the penalty of Termination on the first offense, consistent with the principle of proportionality and its right to protect its business interests.
12. Suppletory Application of the Labor Code

In all matters not specifically covered by this policy, the applicable provisions of the Labor Code of the Philippines (Presidential Decree No. 442, as amended, and renumbered), its Implementing Rules and Regulations, and relevant DOLE Department Orders and jurisprudence shall apply in a suppletory manner.
