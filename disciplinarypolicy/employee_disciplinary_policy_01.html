EMPLOYEE DISCIPLINARY POLICY AND CODE OF CONDUCT

[COMPANY NAME]

SECTION 1: POLICY FUNDAMENTALS (Page 1 of 2)
1. Objective To establish a fair, consistent, and transparent disciplinary system that guides employee behavior, ensures organizational order, and supports professional conduct across all levels of the Company.
2. Rationale (Legal Basis) Ensures compliance with the requirements of **substantive and procedural due process** under the Labor Code of the Philippines and DOLE regulations, protecting both business interests and employee tenure.
3. Effective Date This policy takes effect on **[Date of Implementation]**.
4. Eligibility Applies to **all employees** of [Company Name], regardless of rank, position, or employment status (regular, probationary, or contractual).
SECTION 2: DISCIPLINARY GROUPS AND SANCTIONS
Group Definition Sanction Range (Progressive)
Group I (Minor) Minor infractions affecting personal output or simple rules. 1st: Verbal Warning (documented)
2nd: Written Warning
3rd: 1–3 Days Suspension
4th: 4–7 Days Suspension
5th: Termination for habitual neglect
Group II (Major) Substantially impacts productivity, internal order, or operations. 1st: Written Warning to 7 Days Suspension
2nd: 8–30 Days Suspension
3rd: Termination
Group III (Serious) Just Cause for termination (willful misconduct, breach of trust, crime). Immediate Termination (for the first offense if warranted by severity)
SECTION 3: EXAMPLES OF VIOLATIONS
Group I Examples
  • Tardiness & Undertime
  • Failure to follow dress code
  • Loitering during work hours
  • Minor insubordination or non-compliance
Group II Examples
  • No Call, No Show (NCNS)
  • Unauthorized absence
  • Repeated minor infractions (Habitual Neglect)
  • Gross discourtesy or verbal harassment
Group III Examples
  • Fraud, Theft, or Stealing
  • Sexual Harassment or Physical Assault
  • Falsification of documents
  • Willful Breach of Trust / Unauthorized confidential disclosure

SECTION 4: DUE PROCESS AND SUSPENSION
Twin-Notice Rule for Termination
  1. **First Written Notice (Notice to Explain):** States charges, facts, and grants five (5) calendar days for explanation.
  2. **Opportunity to be Heard:** Employee is given a conference or hearing.
  3. **Second Written Notice (Notice of Decision):** Communicates the final decision and penalty.
Preventive Suspension (PS) A non-punitive measure, maximum 30 days, used if the employee poses a serious threat during investigation.
SECTION 5: PRESCRIPTIVE PERIODS AND APPLICABILITY
Offense Classification Prescriptive Period
Group I Minor Infractions 60 days from discovery
Group II Major Infractions 90 days from discovery
Serious Offenses (Fraud, Crime) Governed by the statutory period of the corresponding legal action.
SECTION 6: SUPPLETORY APPLICATION
In all matters not expressly covered by this Policy, the provisions of the **Labor Code of the Philippines, relevant DOLE issuances, and established labor jurisprudence** shall apply in a suppletory manner.
